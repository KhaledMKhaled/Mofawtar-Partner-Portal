5. Customer Ownership and Recurring Commission Entitlement
==================================================

This is a core rule.

The system must support customer ownership by partner.

When a partner brings a customer and the first request for this customer becomes Activated, the system assigns this customer to that partner as the Customer Owner Partner.

- Draft SR does not start ownership.
- New Request does not start ownership.
- Received does not start ownership.
- Under Activation does not start ownership.
- Failed / Rejected does not start ownership.
- Only Activated starts customer ownership and commission entitlement.

Ownership start date:
- The first activation date for this customer under that partner.

Ownership end date:
- Ownership start date + customer ownership period from the partner profile.
- Example:
  Partner A activates Customer X on 2026-01-01.
  Partner A has Customer Ownership Period = 3 Years.
  Customer X is owned by Partner A from 2026-01-01 until 2028-12-31.
  Starting from 2029-01-01, the ownership is expired and the customer returns to the main company.

During active ownership:
- The customer remains owned by the partner.
- The customer remains visible inside the partner profile.
- The partner is entitled to recurring commissions on all eligible paid transactions related to this customer.
- Eligible transactions include:
  - New Subscription
  - Renewal
  - Upgrade
  - Add-on Package
  - Recurring Payment
  - Any new paid package or paid service related to the same customer
- The commission is calculated every time an eligible paid transaction happens during the ownership period.
- The same Customer 360 Profile must be used. Do not create duplicate customer profiles.

After ownership expires:
- The customer ownership returns to the main company.
- The old partner no longer earns new commissions from this customer.
- The customer remains visible in the old partner profile as historical data only.
- The old partner can see previous requests, previous commissions, previous payments, and previous activity during its ownership period according to permissions.
- The old partner should not see future company-only transactions unless permission allows it.
- No new partner commission should be created for renewals, upgrades, add-ons, recurring payments, or new paid services after the ownership expiry date.
- The main company can manually extend, transfer, or reassign customer ownership if the user has permission.

- If the ownership has expired and returned to the company, partner users cannot create new partner-owned requests unless the company extends or transfers ownership.

11. Order Payment Status
==================================================

Use these payment statuses:

1. Pending Collection Confirmation
Before the Sales user confirms that the full amount was collected.

2. Collected by Sales
The Sales user confirmed that the full amount was collected.

3. Held by Partner
The amount is now under the partner’s responsibility.

4. Net Amount Due to Company
The system calculated the net amount that the partner must deliver to the main company after deducting partner commission.

5. Received by Company
The company accountant confirmed that the net amount was received.

6. Settled
The payment has been fully settled.

7. Refunded / Cancelled
Used when the payment is cancelled or refunded.

Payment status flow:
- Pending Collection Confirmation -> Collected by Sales
- Collected by Sales -> Held by Partner
- Held by Partner -> Net Amount Due to Company
- Net Amount Due to Company -> Received by Company
- Received by Company -> Settled
- Any relevant stage can move to Refunded / Cancelled with reason and permission

Settlement rule between partner and company:
- The partner delivers the net amount to the company after deducting partner commission.
- Always display:
  - Full customer amount before deduction
  - Partner commission amount
  - Net amount due to company after deduction

Calculation:
Full customer amount = Final price after tax
Commission base = paid amount after discounts before tax
Partner commission amount = commission base * partner commission %
Net amount due to company = full customer amount - partner commission amount

Example without tax:
Package amount = 1000
Partner commission = 20% = 200
Net amount due to company = 800

Example with tax:
Item price before tax = 1000
Tax = 14% = 140
Full customer amount = 1140
Partner commission = 20% of 1000 = 200
Net amount due to company = 1140 - 200 = 940

The company receives the net amount from the partner.
